How can you contribute?

  • Reduce Plastic Use: Opt for reusable bags, bottles, and containers to cut down on plastic waste.

  • Proper Waste Disposal: Recycle, compost, and dispose of waste responsibly to prevent litter from reaching waterways.

  • Choose Eco-Friendly Products: Select biodegradable and non-toxic household products to reduce chemical runoff

  • Participate in Cleanups: Join local beach or river cleanups to directly remove trash from ecosystems.

  • Limit Seafood Consumption: Choose sustainable seafood to reduce overfishing and support healthier ocean habitats.

  • Conserve Water: Reduce water usage to limit runoff and pollutants entering the ocean.

    How will ocean polution impact you?

    Ocean pollution will directly affect our generation, bringing health risks as toxins and plastics enter our food and our bodies. It could also limit future job opportunities in marine-related fields, harm beach and water activities we enjoy, and accelerate climate change as oceans lose their ability to absorb CO₂. With damaged ecosystems and fewer thriving coastal areas, we’ll likely face economic and environmental challenges. But by choosing sustainable habits and becoming advocates for cleaner oceans, we have the power to shape a healthier future for ourselves and the planet.
